Child Pornography in India

Everyone talks about pornography but only a few understand how children are suffering from these issues. Don’t worry our agencies are now 100% capable to tackle and eliminate child pornography. Let’s continue this healthy reading.

Pornography means the depiction of any content, whether printed or visual, that contains manifest narration, demonstration, or display of sexual activities, sexual organs, or any other obscene material with an intention to trigger the sexual excitement of the viewers. We call it child pornography when children are involved in these types of activities. It’s like a horror dream for children & victims.

Child pornography promotes and encourages exploitation, trafficking, and violence towards children. This is the reason why child pornography is considered a heinous crime.

Everyone around the globe has access to the Internet. It has become so simple and easy for people to gain access to child pornography, and unfortunately, the exchange of this type of content has reached its maximum. This has allowed a considerable increase in the production of child pornography. Some photographs that were secretly taken by criminals without their consent are mostly used in these heinous crimes. Criminals are able to exchange these child pornographic materials with each other, advices are also exchanged on their evil practice. There are forums and chats (especially Telegram, Signal and WhatsApp are a hub of these types of contents) where information and files are exchanged on how to commit these crimes without leaving any evidence.

Impact of child pornography:


POCSO Act

To combat child sexual abuse cases, the Indian Government has brought in a special law, named, The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act. This act is enacted for protecting Children’s under the special law, Section 13, Section 14 and Section 15.

Indian Penal Code, 1860 and Information Technology Act, 2000

Indian Penal Code does not deal with child pornography directly, but it prohibits the display of obscene material to young persons. The Section 293 and Section 67B of the IT Act punishes the act of publishing, transmitting, creating, collecting, browsing, downloading, advertising, promoting, exchanging, distributing, recording, etc. of any material in electronic form which shows sexually explicit activity engaging a child.
